Kinematic Mirror Mount

Kinematic Optic Mount · Precision Mirror Mount · Kinematic Rotation Mount

SKU: SET-KMM-025

Accommodates Ø25.0 to Ø25.4 mm (1") circular optical elements · Minimum optic thickness: 3 mm

Designed for applications where precision, repeatability, and mechanical stability are essential.

This Kinematic mirror mount is designed for accurate alignment and stable positioning of Ø25.0–25.4 mm (1") optical components in laboratory, research, and industrial optical systems. The mount combines high mechanical rigidity with fine angular control, making it ideal for demanding optical alignment applications.

Features

  • Accommodates Ø25.0 to Ø25.4 mm (1") circular optical elements
  • Minimum optic thickness: 3 mm
  • Nylon-tipped setscrew with 7 mm deep optic cavity for secure, stress-free optic retention
  • Clear aperture: 24.0 mm (94% of optic diameter)
  • Two 1/4"-100 TPI precision adjuster screws providing ±4° tip and tilt adjustment
  • Angular resolution: 6 mrad (0.4°) per revolution
  • Sensitivity: 4 arc-sec
  • High-quality removable adjustment knobs
  • Two counterbored M4 through holes for left- and right-hand mounting orientations
  • Precision-machined aluminium construction with durable black anodized finish
  • Mechanical Design
  • Fabricated from high-grade aluminium and finished with premium black anodization, the mount offers excellent rigidity, corrosion resistance, and long-term stability. The robust design minimizes drift and maintains alignment accuracy in demanding laboratory environments.
  • Removable adjustment knobs enable convenient manual alignment and can be detached to expose hex sockets for tool-assisted fine tuning, making the mount suitable for compact, enclosed, or vibration-sensitive optical setups.
  • Optic Retention
  • The mount employs a three-point optic retention system using a nylon-tipped locking screw to securely hold delicate optical components while minimizing mounting-induced stress.
  • An integrated rotational mechanism with locking provision allows controlled rotational adjustment of the optic without compromising alignment stability.

Applications

  • Laser beam steering and alignment
  • Mirror and beamsplitter mounting
  • Interferometry systems
  • Optical instrumentation and metrology
  • Educational and research laboratories
  • Quantum optics and photonics experiments
  • Industrial optical assemblies
