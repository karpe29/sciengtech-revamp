Motion and Positioning

Rotation Mount

360° Precision Rotation Mount · Continuous Rotation Mount · Rotational Stage

SKU: SET-RM-025

Designed for Ø25.4 mm (1") optical components · Maximum optic thickness: 12 mm

Designed for laboratories and optical systems requiring precise angular adjustment, secure locking, repeatable positioning, and long-term mechanical stability.

SciEngTech Rotation Mount is designed for accurate angular positioning and smooth continuous rotation of Ø25.4 mm (1") optical components. Its compact, lockable design provides backlash-free operation, making it ideal for polarization optics, waveplates, filters, and other applications requiring precise rotational alignment.

The mount incorporates an SM1-threaded optic holder and a finely engraved 360° scale, enabling repeatable angular positioning while maintaining excellent mechanical stability.

Features

  • Designed for Ø25.4 mm (1") optical components
  • Maximum optic thickness: 12 mm
  • Enables smooth rotation of optics within fixed lens tube systems
  • Full 360° engraved scale marked in 2° increments
  • SM1 (1.035"-40) internal threading
  • Compatible with standard SM1 retaining rings
  • Independently rotatable dial face for convenient angle referencing
  • M4 tapped mounting hole on the bottom
  • Precision-machined aluminium construction
  • Black hard-anodized finish for durability and reduced stray reflections
  • Mechanical Design
  • The rotation mount provides smooth, continuous, and backlash-free angular adjustment for precision optical alignment applications. The integrated locking mechanism securely maintains the selected angular position, ensuring excellent repeatability and long-term stability.
  • The independently rotatable dial allows users to set a reference angle without disturbing the mounted optic, simplifying calibration and alignment procedures.

Applications

  • Polarizer and analyzer alignment
  • Waveplate and retarder positioning
  • Optical filter orientation
  • Laser polarization control
  • Precision optical instrumentation
  • Educational and research laboratories
  • Photonics and quantum optics experiments
